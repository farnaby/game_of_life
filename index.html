Conway's Game of Life

How to Play

Create a pattern by selecting squares on the board. Then click "Next Generation" as often as you like and watch evolution play its game. Once it gets boring, feel free to play god and change the natural course of life by toggling squares on the board again.

The Rules of Evolution

Evolution is governed by four rules:

  1. Each square has eight neighbours. If one square is alive and two or three of its neighbours are also alive, it survives.
  2. If less than two of its neighbours are alive, it dies from loneliness.
  3. If more than three neighbours are alive, it dies due to overpopulation.
  4. When there is an empty field with exactly three living neighbours, a new child is born.
